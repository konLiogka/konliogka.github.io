This is a mini portable guitar amp powered by a 9V battery. The Sapphire Lite Amp schematic was designed by Idle Wrench @dylan159.9855 on discord. It is a simple amplifier that utilizes the chips TL072 for amplifying the sound and LM386 as power amplifier for the driver, which I sourced from some old logitech speakers.

How it works

First of all, the dual op-amp TL072 is configured as non-inverting so the signal enters from the "+" input. A reference voltage of 4.5V is used so as to provide a constant source for biasing the op-amp. Two diodes are used as flyback diodes to protect the circuit from over voltages. The GAIN potentiometer is what makes the sound more "metal" as it pushes the waveform to its limits, effectively cutting the top.

At the OUT1 of the TL072, there's a TONE potentiometer for adjusting the tone. If there's high resistance, it allows higher frequencies to pass. The signal goes once again into the second op-amp of the TL072, it doesnt amplify the sound but acts as a voltage follower to isolate the signal from load currents.

The amplified and buffered signal is then sent to the power amplifier LM386. The built-in gain is not needed. Before sending the signal to the speaker it is VERY important to use a resistor in parallel to the speaker that matches the impedance of the speaker, as well as an electrolytic capacitor as a means to not short the amplifier's DC output to ground which makes them overheat and burn out.
